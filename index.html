PHYS 2240

Physics III with Calculus Lab

Python Primer

Learn basic Python tools for analyzing and visualizing data.

Brownian Motion

Calculate Avogadro's number from measurements of random walk behavior of particles suspended in fluid, inspired by Einstein's 1905 paper.

Waves on a String

Use standing wave patterns to measure the linear mass density of a string made of unknown material.

Acoustic Resonance

Determine the speed of sound in air by analyzing the conditions under which resonance amplifies standing sound waves in a tube.

The Classical Doppler Effect

Use measurements of frequency shifts from a moving sound source to determine its emitted frequency via the Doppler effect.

Mirrors & The Law of Reflection

Apply the law of reflection to investigate image formation and determine the focal lengths of curved mirrors.

Lenses & The Law of Refraction

Use the law of refraction to determine the focal lengths of lenses and measure the index of refraction of water.

Interference & Diffraction of Light

Analyze interference and diffraction patterns of visible light to explore its wave nature and measure its wavelength.

Thin Film Interference

Observe Newton’s rings to investigate thin-film interference and determine the radius of curvature of a lens from the resulting fringe pattern.

The Relativistic Doppler Effect

Analyze the relativistic Doppler shift in galactic spectra to determine the Hubble constant and explore the expansion of the universe.

Relativistic Energy & Momentum

Use conservation of relativistic energy and momentum to analyze LHC collision data and estimate the mass of the Higgs boson.

Blackbody Radiation

Apply the principles of blackbody radiation to estimate the surface temperature of the Sun from its observed spectrum.

The Photoelectric Effect

Investigate the photoelectric effect to determine Planck’s constant and explore the quantization of light.

Atomic Emission Spectra

Analyze atomic emission spectra to measure the Rydberg constant and probe the quantized energy levels of hydrogen.

Nuclear Radiation

Measure how different types of nuclear radiation interact with shielding to identify them and explore their properties.
